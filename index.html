Maker Meetup: Workshops is a one day event for software developers interested in hardware world. On Saturday, 26th October, 80 people will gather in Warsaw to BUILD things. We will teach you and help you with your first steps with Arduino, 3D Printing, micro-location and Low-Energy Bluetooth. This is a non-profit event organized by community, for community. It's also a rehersal meetup before Makerland :)

Saturday, 26th Oct
Gamma Factory
Warsaw, Poland

Get tickets

Workshops

All attendees will be divided into groups of 10-15 people. Our amazing workshop leaders will prepare for you 5 workshops stations. Workshop on each station will take up to one hour and then you move on to the next one. In 6 hours (+breakfast & lunch) you will build your own Arduino for 25 PLN, learn everything about printing and modeling in 3D, building Lego Mindstorms robots, micro-location and Low-Energy Bluetooth. Of course, all required equipements and hardware will be provided and are included in the ticket. Only thing you have to bring with you is a proper attitiude and your laptop!

Learn more about our fantastic workshops and workshop leaders:

Bluetooth Low Energy & Micro-location

Szymon Niemczura is a co-founder of technology startup Kontakt that is building micro-location solutions with Bluetooth Low Energy Beacons. During his workshop you will learn how to control your lights or lock your computer automatically when you leave your room.

Build your own Arduino

Lukasz is an owner of WeMakeRobots, site focused on electronics, design and creation of mobile robots. In his workshop he will teach you how to build your own Arduino for as little as 25 PLN.

Questions you may ask us:

What do I have to bring with me?
  • 1) A laptop. Best are those using Linux or MacOSX.
  • A ticket, and ID
Do I have to be a programmer to attend?
No. But you need a working knowledge about programming, meaning you wrote a function or two in your life before.
Why workshops are called stations?
In their nature they are asynchronous, so you can join and live when it suits you. This means you can choose in which order you want to visit stations. You decide.
What is the formula behind stations?
Stations are assisted self-learning experience. You can sit whenever you want, and start going through the prepared tutorial. You can always talk to station leader, ask for his assistance etc.
What if I want to join a station that is full?
Just come little bit later
I already know everything about Internet of Things,3d printing etc. is this an event for me?
Nope.
Is MakerMeetup: Workshops a competition?
No :) You come just for fun and learning.
Will there be food?
Yes :)
Will there be more MakerMeetup: Workshops?
Workshops are tough to organize, so we can do this max twice a year. But we want to have monthly MakerMeetup in Warsaw, starting November
Maybe, let's see how this works.
