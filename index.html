WHAT IS MAKER MEETUP? WORKSHOPS TICKETS FAQ TEAM TL;DR

Maker Meetup: Workshops is a one day event for software developers interested in hardware world. On Saturday, 16th November, 80 people will gather in Warsaw to BUILD things. We will teach you and help you with your first steps with Arduino, 3D Printing, micro-location and Low-Energy Bluetooth. This is a non-profit event organized by community, for community. It's also a rehersal meetup before Makerland :)

Saturday, 16th Nov
Warsaw, Poland

Get tickets

Workshops

It's not going to be like in a school. Our amazing workshop leaders will prepare for you 5 workshops stations. Workshop on each station will take up to one hour and then you move on to the next one. They are also asynchronous which means that you can spend on each station as much or as little time as you need. In 6 hours (+breakfast & lunch) you will build your own Arduino for 25 PLN, learn about printing and modeling in 3D, building Lego Mindstorms robots, micro-location and Low-Energy Bluetooth. Of course, all required equipements and hardware will be provided and are included in the ticket. Only thing you have to bring with you is a proper attitiude and your laptop!

Learn more about our fantastic workshops and workshop leaders:

Bluetooth Low Energy & Micro-location

Szymon Niemczura is a co-founder of technology startup Kontakt that is building micro-location solutions with Bluetooth Low Energy Beacons. During his workshop you will learn how to control your lights or lock your computer automatically when you leave your room.

Build your own Arduino

Lukasz Jakobiec is an owner of WeMakeRobots, site focused on electronics, design and creation of mobile robots. In his workshop he will teach you how to build your own Arduino for as little as 25 PLN.
Of course, when you're done, you can take it home!

3D Printing

Michal Fraczek is a community manager of Materialination, the biggest comunity of 3D printing fans. His 3D printing workshops will show you how to transform your models into physical objects.
Everything you print during workshops belongs to you :)

3D Modeling

Miłosz Dąbrowski is a product/industrial designer at UAUproject & member of LOKO team. 3D tinkerer, maker & design adventurer. He will show you how to transform your 2D ideas into 3D models that you can print during or after Maker Meetup!

Questions you may ask us:

What do I have to bring with me?
1) A laptop. Best are those using Linux or Mac OS X
2) A ticket or ID
Do I have to be a programmer to attend?
No but you at least need a working knowledge about programming, meaning you wrote a function or two in your life before.
Why workshops are called stations?
In their nature they are asynchronous, so you can join and leave when it suits you. This means you can choose in which order you want to visit stations or spend most time on those which interest you the most. You decide.
What is the formula behind stations?
Stations are assisted self-learning experience. You can sit whenever you want, and start going through the prepared tutorial. You can always talk to station leader and ask for his assistance.
What if I want to join a station that is full?
Just come a little bit later, no worries!
I already know everything about Internet of Things, 3D printing etc. is this an event for me?
Nope, you'll probably be bored. But hey, it'd be awesome if you want to help out as station leader! Make sure to get in touch with us: meetup@makerland.org
Is MakerMeetup: Workshops a competition?
No :) You come just for fun and learning.
Will there be food?
Yes. And it'll be healthy & tasty!
Will there be more MakerMeetup: Workshops?
Workshops are tough to organize, so we can do this max twice a year. But we are planning monthly MakerMeetups in Warsaw, starting December 2013.
